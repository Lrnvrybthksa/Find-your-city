Understanding Your Results

What Primary, Secondary, and Third actually mean — and how to use them.

🥇
Primary Archetype
Your Core Drive

Your Primary archetype is the foundation — the environment type that most consistently energizes you and aligns with how you naturally live, think, and recharge. It's the non-negotiable. A city that misses this will always feel subtly off, no matter how good everything else looks on paper.

Think of it as your baseline requirement. If you're a Quiet Observer, you need beauty and unhurried pace — put yourself in a city that's chaotic and overwhelming and no amount of good restaurants or career opportunity will make up for that constant drain. Your Primary is the lens through which everything else gets filtered.

When you look at your city recommendations, the Primary list is the most important one. These are cities where the core conditions of your life — climate, pace, social energy, environment — are most likely to match who you are. Start here.

🥈
Secondary Archetype
Your Daily Texture

Your Secondary archetype shapes the texture of your daily life — not your core drive, but what needs to be present around you for things to feel right on a Tuesday afternoon. It's the layer below the surface. You might not notice it when it's there, but you'll definitely feel it when it's missing.

A good city match will satisfy both your Primary and Secondary. For example, a Quiet Observer with a Coastal Creative secondary doesn't just need calm and beauty — they need it near water. An Ambitious Innovator with an Urban Intellectual secondary needs a fast city, but one with depth and culture, not just hustle.

When comparing cities in your results, look for ones that appear across both your Primary and Secondary lists — those overlaps are your strongest matches. They're cities that satisfy more than one dimension of who you are.

🥉
Third Archetype
Your Quiet Craving

Your Third archetype is often the most surprising — and the most revealing. It represents something you may be moving toward, quietly missing, or subconsciously drawn to without fully understanding why. It's not your dominant trait, but it's a real part of you that shows up in your answers.

People often find that their Third archetype explains cities they've always been attracted to but couldn't articulate why. Maybe you've always wanted to visit Oaxaca or Kyoto — and your Third archetype being Romantic Aesthetic finally makes that make sense. It adds nuance and depth to your overall picture.

Don't dismiss your Third result. It often points to what you need more of — the dimension of city life you've been underweighting in your choices so far. Cities that satisfy all three archetypes are rare, but they exist. And when you find one, it tends to feel like home almost immediately.

Why this matters

More than a quiz — a shift in how you see the world

Most people never question whether the city they live in — or the cities they visit — actually fit who they are. They go where jobs take them, where friends moved, where flights were cheap. Find Your City starts from a different place: that the right environment is one of the most powerful forces in your life, shaping your energy, your creativity, your social world, and even your health. The wrong city drains you quietly. The right one makes everything feel a little easier. This quiz is designed to help you figure out which is which — for you specifically.

Beyond the obvious bucket list cities, the world is full of extraordinary places that most people never discover simply because no one told them to look. Some of the best cities for you might be ones you've never heard of — smaller, more affordable, more alive in the ways that matter to your personality. A coastal town in Portugal. A mountain city in New Zealand. A post-industrial arts hub in Eastern Europe. Cities where the culture, pace, climate, and social energy align so closely with how you're built that they feel like coming home — even the first time you visit. This quiz surfaces those cities by matching them to your archetype, not just the mainstream rankings.

At its core, Find Your City is really about self-awareness. Understanding what kind of environment energizes you versus drains you. Knowing whether you need mountains or coastlines, density or space, solitude or social buzz. That self-knowledge doesn't just help you travel better — it helps you live better. It makes you more intentional about where you spend your time and money, more confident in decisions about where to move or relocate, and more likely to find the kind of daily life that genuinely excites you. The cities are the destination. Knowing yourself is the real discovery.
